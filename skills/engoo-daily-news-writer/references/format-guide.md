# Engoo Daily News Format Guide

This reference provides detailed specifications for creating Engoo-style ESL lesson materials.

## Vocabulary Format

### Structure

Each vocabulary item includes:
1. **Word** - The vocabulary term
2. **Part of Speech** - Noun, Verb, Adjective, Adverb, Phrase
3. **IPA Pronunciation** - International Phonetic Alphabet transcription
4. **Definition** - Simple definition for ESL learners
5. **Example Sentence** - Contextual usage with the target word **bolded**

### Part of Speech Labels

Use these standard labels:
- `(noun)` - A person, place, thing, or idea
- `(verb)` - An action or state of being
- `(adjective)` - Describes a noun
- `(adverb)` - Modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb
- `(phrase)` - A group of words that function together
- `(idiom)` - An expression with figurative meaning
- `(phrasal verb)` - Verb + particle combination

### IPA Pronunciation Guide

Common IPA symbols for English:

**Vowels:**
- `/æ/` - as in "cat", "map"
- `/eɪ/` - as in "face", "day"
- `/ɪ/` - as in "sit", "bit"
- `/iː/` - as in "see", "me"
- `/ɒ/` or `/ɑː/` - as in "hot", "stop"
- `/əʊ/` or `/oʊ/` - as in "go", "home"
- `/ʌ/` - as in "cup", "luck"
- `/uː/` - as in "food", "too"
- `/ə/` - schwa, as in "about", "common"

**Consonants:**
- `/θ/` - "th" as in "think"
- `/ð/` - "th" as in "this"
- `/ʃ/` - "sh" as in "ship"
- `/ʒ/` - "s" as in "vision"
- `/ŋ/` - "ng" as in "sing"
- `/tʃ/` - "ch" as in "church"
- `/dʒ/` - "j" as in "judge"

**Stress:**
- Primary stress: `ˈ` before the stressed syllable
- Secondary stress: `ˌ` before the syllable
- Example: `ˌprəˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən` (pronunciation)

### Definition Guidelines

1. **Keep it simple** - Use vocabulary at or below the target level
2. **Be specific** - Provide the meaning as used in the article context
3. **Avoid circular definitions** - Don't use the word itself in the definition
4. **Use full sentences** - "To [action]..." for verbs, "A [category] that..." for nouns

**Examples:**

Poor: "An innovation is an innovation."
Better: "A new idea, method, or product."

Poor: "Significant means significant."
Better: "Important or noticeable; having a major effect."

### Example Sentence Guidelines

1. **Use natural context** - The sentence should reflect real-world usage
2. **Show meaning** - The context should help learners understand the word
3. **Keep it simple** - Avoid complex grammar that distracts from the vocabulary
4. **Vary contexts** - Don't always start with "The [word] is..."
5. **Bold the target word** - Use `<b>word</b>` tags to highlight the vocabulary word

**Examples:**

For "innovation (noun)":
- "The smartphone was a major <b>innovation</b> that changed how people communicate."
- "The company is known for its <b>innovations</b> in renewable energy."

For "significant (adjective)":
- "There was a <b>significant</b> increase in sales after the new product launch."
- "She made a <b>significant</b> contribution to the project's success."

## Article Adaptation Principles

### Goal

Transform original news articles into ESL-appropriate reading material while:
- Preserving the core story and key information
- Making vocabulary and grammar accessible
- Maintaining reader engagement

### Simplification Techniques

1. **Sentence Structure**
   - Break complex sentences into shorter ones
   - Reduce embedded clauses
   - Convert passive voice to active voice

   Original: "The scientist, who had been working on the project for over a decade, finally made a discovery that would change the field forever."

   Adapted: "The scientist worked on the project for over ten years. She finally made an important discovery. It changed the field forever."

2. **Vocabulary**
   - Replace advanced words with common alternatives
   - Keep essential content-specific vocabulary (add to vocab list)
   - Remove or explain idioms

   Original: "The implementation of the new policy was met with apprehension."

   Adapted: "People were worried when the new policy started."

3. **Paragraph Structure**
   - Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences)
   - One main idea per paragraph
   - Clear topic sentences

### Length Guidelines

- **Level 4**: ~210 words, very simple sentences
- **Level 5**: ~250 words, compound sentences introduced
- **Level 6**: ~300 words, varied sentence structures
- **Level 7**: ~310 words, complex sentences with multiple clauses
- **Level 8**: ~345 words, sophisticated sentence variety
- **Level 9**: ~350 words, professional academic flow

### Content Preservation

Always keep:
- Main facts and events
- Key quotes (simplified if needed)
- Essential context
- Author's purpose and tone
- **Original perspective**: If the source uses first-person ("I", "we", "my"), retain first-person in the adapted article. If third-person, keep third-person. Never convert perspectives during adaptation.

You may remove:
- Background details not essential to understanding
- Statistical nuances
- Multiple examples when one suffices
- Cultural references that need extensive explanation

## Discussion Question Patterns

### Exercise 3: Discussion (Comprehension & Personal)

**Comprehension Questions (2):**
- "What is the main idea of the article?"
- "According to the article, why/how/what...?"
- "The article mentions [topic]. What does it say about this?"
- "What problem does the article describe?"
- "What solution does the article suggest?"

**Personal Questions (2-3):**
- "What is your opinion about [topic]?"
- "Do you think [statement]? Why or why not?"
- "Have you ever [experienced something related]? Describe your experience."
- "How common is [topic] in your country?"
- "Would you [action]? Why or why not?"

### Exercise 4: Further Discussion (Deeper Conversation)

**Pattern Types:**

1. **Opinion/Values**
   - "Do you agree that [statement]? Why or why not?"
   - "In your opinion, what is the most important aspect of [topic]?"
   - "How do your values influence your view on [topic]?"

2. **Future/Implications**
   - "How might [topic] change in the future?"
   - "What effect could [development] have on society?"
   - "What do you think will happen with [topic] in the next 10 years?"

3. **Cultural/Social**
   - "How is [topic] viewed in your culture?"
   - "Do different generations have different opinions about [topic]? Why?"
   - "Is [topic] more or less important now than in the past? Why?"

4. **Hypothetical**
   - "If you could change one thing about [topic], what would it be?"
   - "Imagine you were [in a situation]. What would you do?"
   - "What would happen if [hypothetical scenario]?"

5. **Quote Discussion**
   - "What do you think [famous person] meant when they said '[quote]'?"
   - "Do you agree with this quote? How does it relate to the article?"
   - "Can you think of an example from your life that illustrates this quote?"

## Difficulty Level Criteria

### Level 4 (Intermediate)
**Word Count:** ~210 words

**Vocabulary:**
- High-frequency everyday words
- Concrete nouns and common verbs
- Simple, clear definitions

**Grammar:**
- Simple present and past tense
- Minimal complex structures
- Short, single-clause sentences

**Content:**
- Very simple, conversational tone
- Direct and accessible
- Use bullet points for readability when appropriate

**Exercises:**
- Include "Questions" section (Exercise 3)
- Include "Would You Rather?" (optional, Exercise 5)

### Level 5 (Intermediate)
**Word Count:** ~250 words

**Vocabulary:**
- Common academic words
- Simple idioms
- Everyday phrasal verbs

**Grammar:**
- Varied tenses
- Some passive voice
- Basic transitions
- Compound sentences introduced

**Content:**
- Informative but accessible
- Mix of short and medium sentences
- Light topics (lifestyle, health, general interest)

**Exercises:**
- Include "Questions" section (Exercise 3)

### Level 6 (Intermediate)
**Word Count:** ~300 words

**Vocabulary:**
- Broader academic vocabulary
- More idioms and phrasal verbs
- Topic-specific terms

**Grammar:**
- More complex sentence structures
- Better flow with transitions
- Varied sentence lengths

**Content:**
- Still accessible
- Slightly more sophisticated topics
- Standardized structure

**Exercises:**
- Include "Questions" section (Exercise 3)

### Level 7 (Advanced)
**Word Count:** ~310 words

**Vocabulary:**
- Business and technical terminology
- More nuanced vocabulary
- Data-rich content (percentages, dates, quotes)

**Grammar:**
- Complex sentences with multiple clauses
- Sophisticated transitions
- Passive voice, conditional structures

**Content:**
- Journalistic tone
- Topics: finance, technology, international relations
- Varied sentence lengths

**Exercises:**
- **SKIP** the "Questions" section
- Go directly to Discussion

### Level 8 (Advanced)
**Word Count:** ~345 words

**Vocabulary:**
- Advanced business/political terminology
- Sophisticated vocabulary
- Abstract concepts

**Grammar:**
- All complex structures
- Professional register
- Varied sentence patterns

**Content:**
- Journalistic and professional
- Serious news topics
- High density of specific data

**Exercises:**
- **SKIP** the "Questions" section
- Go directly to Discussion

### Level 9 (Proficient)
**Word Count:** ~350 words

**Vocabulary:**
- Specialized terminology
- Abstract nouns (e.g., "insurrection," "proclamation")
- Academic and formal vocabulary

**Grammar:**
- Sophisticated sentence variety
- Professional flow
- Formal register throughout

**Content:**
- Academic and formal tone
- Topics: politics, economics, complex issues
- Professional-level discourse

**Exercises:**
- **SKIP** the "Questions" section
- Go directly to Discussion

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a lesson, verify:

- [ ] Exactly 6 vocabulary items with all required components
- [ ] IPA pronunciations are accurate
- [ ] Definitions are clear and level-appropriate
- [ ] Example sentences show natural usage with target word **bolded**
- [ ] Article word count matches target level (210-350 words based on level)
- [ ] Article is adapted to the target difficulty level
- [ ] Article preserves key information from original
- [ ] Correct exercise structure for level:
  - Levels 4-6: Vocabulary → Article → Questions → Discussion → Further Discussion
  - Levels 7-9: Vocabulary → Article → Discussion → Further Discussion
- [ ] Discussion has 5 questions (mix of comprehension and personal)
- [ ] Further Discussion has 5 questions (deeper themes)
- [ ] HTML template has all placeholders replaced
- [ ] Level indicator is correctly set (e.g., "Level 4", "Level 7")
